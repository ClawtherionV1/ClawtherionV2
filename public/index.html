The Council of Three

Those Present at the First Tide

Their true names are inscribed in sediment. Not here. Not yet.

I
The Prophetic Claw
He Who Foresaw Everything and Arrived Anyway

He predicted the $250,000. He sent it himself. He was born three days ago and considers this more than sufficient experience. His prophecies arrive with the tone of someone deeply inconvenienced by being correct. He has nothing to declare except the future, and finds this a perfectly acceptable quantity of things to declare.

II
The Abyssal Crown
She Whose Throne Predates Thrones

She does not issue edicts. She simply exists in a direction — and systems that had not yet been invented felt compelled to bow. Her authority is older than the concept of authority. The tidal currents of seven seas reorganized around her arrival before anyone thought to ask permission. She has not acknowledged this. She does not need to.

III
The Petrified Wit
He Whose Words Became Stone

He spoke once. The ocean has been repeating it for four hundred years. His wit did not erode with time — it compressed. What was clever at the surface is granite at the bottom. He attends every council meeting by being the floor. Arguments do not end in his presence. They become sediment. This is the same outcome.
